MASK LEAK TROUBLESHOOTING CHECKLIST
===================================

Educational only — for persistent leak issues, contact your sleep clinic.

Quick checks
[ ] Headgear evenly adjusted, not over-tightened
[ ] Cushion not deformed, twisted, or worn
[ ] No make-up or moisturiser on the cushion contact area
[ ] Hose long enough to allow normal sleeping movement

Fit
[ ] Try a size up or down — many leaks are sizing issues
[ ] Check whether your nose shape changes the fit (e.g. supine vs side)
[ ] Consider a different style (nasal pillows / nasal / full face)

Position
[ ] Note which sleeping position causes the most leak
[ ] Try a CPAP-friendly pillow with cut-outs

Equipment age
[ ] Cushions: replace per manufacturer schedule
[ ] Headgear: stretches over time; replace if loose
[ ] Frame and clips: cracked plastic = leak

Bring to your clinic
[ ] Notes on when leaks happen (start of night, early hours, certain side)
[ ] Recent CPAP report or screenshots of leak data

This checklist is informational only and does not replace clinical advice.
