# Brand brief — Sleep Apnoea Companion

A short brief to help you (the designer) come up with a name shortlist and a
logo for a free educational web app. Everything you need to understand the
product, audience, voice and visual system is below. Working name in the
codebase is *Aimsphere*; treat it as a placeholder, not a constraint.

## The product in one paragraph

A free, calm, plain-English web app for people living with — or newly
diagnosed with — sleep apnoea. It explains the condition without jargon,
walks beginners through CPAP therapy, and lets users upload a ZIP of their
CPAP machine's data to get a clean visual dashboard (usage, AHI, leak,
pressure trends) plus a print-ready PDF for their sleep clinic. There is no
login wall, no "premium" tier, and the medical pages are kept entirely
separate from a small affiliate products section that helps fund the site.

## What it does

- Educational guides on sleep apnoea, CPAP therapy and common first-month
  problems.
- ZIP upload + auto-detection of CPAP data formats; produces a per-night
  dashboard with charts, plain-English explanations and downloadable
  PDF/CSV reports.
- Optional journal where users log sleep quality, mask comfort, dry mouth,
  alcohol etc. and look for patterns.
- A separate, clearly disclosed product recommendations page (masks,
  hoses, filters, accessories).

## Who it's for

- People who have just been diagnosed and feel overwhelmed.
- People already using CPAP who want to understand their numbers.
- Partners, friends and family helping someone manage the condition.
- Skews 35–70, mostly UK/EN-GB but international.
- Tech comfort: average. They can zip a folder; they cannot install OSCAR
  on Linux.

## Tone and personality

Five words to anchor any naming or visual decision:

1. **Calm** — sleep apnoea is anxious territory; the brand should feel like
   a deep breath, not a defibrillator.
2. **Honest** — no scaremongering, no commercial slipperiness. The site
   refuses to suggest pressure changes and constantly defers to clinicians.
3. **Editorial** — closer to a thoughtful magazine than a hospital website.
4. **Warm but credible** — friendly, but the user has to trust us with
   medical-adjacent content.
5. **Unfussy** — clarity first, then beauty, then cleverness. Never the
   other way round.

Words to **avoid** in naming and copy: *clinic, medical, diagnose, cure,
score, scan, AI, smart, optimise, hack, tracker (tired)*. We are not
diagnostic software; we are a companion.

## Visual system already in place

The current site uses a calm cream + watercolour palette inspired by an
Aimsphere/SleepMania reference (loosely magazine-meets-wellness). A logo
should sit naturally inside this world.

The palette logic: cream + deep teal carry the structural weight. The six
watercolour accents only ever appear as soft, blurred radial gradients —
think pastel watercolour blooms, not solid swatches. The hero on the home
page layers five of them at low opacity over a cream base.

### Typography

- **Display:** Fraunces (variable serif, 300–500 weights). High contrast,
  warm, slightly editorial. Used for headlines, brand wordmark, and the
  big numbers in dashboard metric cards.
- **Body:** Inter. Clean, neutral, very legible from 14–18px. Used for
  body, UI labels and microcopy.
- **Eyebrows / labels / buttons:** Inter, uppercase, `letter-spacing:
  0.18em–0.22em`, weight 600. Always small-caps style — never sentence case.
- **Buttons:** pill-shaped, outlined by default; filled deep-teal for the
  single primary CTA on a page.

### Motifs

- **Soft watercolour blobs** as the only real illustration style — round,
  layered, blurred edges, never hard.
- **Thin gold-grey hairlines** (`#C9C0B0`) as section separators.
- **Pill / circular shapes** rather than sharp rectangles; corner radius
  is generous (16–24px) on cards, fully rounded on buttons.
- **Plenty of whitespace.** The page should feel slow and considered.

## What I need from you

### 1. A name shortlist (5–8 candidates)

Working name *Aimsphere* is fine but unowned and probably doesn't ladder up
to "calm + honest + editorial". Looking for something that:

- Reads as a **companion / guide / quiet helper**, not as software or a
  medical device.
- Could comfortably sit on the spine of a paperback as well as in an app
  store.
- Works in British English without sounding either too clinical or too
  start-up.
- Plays well with a wordmark in Fraunces (avoid awkward letterforms /
  characters that the serif handles badly).
- Has a plausible story for a 1–2 line tagline.

For each candidate I'd love a one-line reasoning, a tagline option, and a
quick check on `.com` / `.co` / `.health` availability.

### 2. A logo system

- **Wordmark** in Fraunces (or a custom serif close in spirit). Light to
  regular weight, generous letter-spacing.
- **Mark** that can stand alone in a 32×32 favicon as well as a 200×200
  social avatar. Current placeholder is a tiny watercolour-blob circle —
  please feel free to replace.
- The mark should hint at one of: rest, breath, a quiet horizon, a
  crescent, a stilled circle. Not a pillow, not a moon-face, not a
  stylised "Z", and definitely not a stethoscope or lungs.
- Deliver in SVG, plus PNG @1x/@2x for social, plus a single-colour
  variant for the dark teal feature blocks and the deep teal footer.

### 3. Tone-of-voice sample (optional)

If naming makes you think the site should also reword its hero ("Sleep is
the most important part of your day."), I'd welcome 2–3 alternative hero
lines in the same brief.

## Constraints worth knowing

- **British English** throughout — *apnoea*, not *apnea*; *colour*,
  *paediatric*, *organise*.
- **Medical disclaimer** is repeated on every relevant page. The brand
  must never imply diagnosis, prescription, or pressure-change advice.
- **Affiliate-funded but ad-free**. The brand should feel trustworthy
  enough that "we earn a small commission on some product links" doesn't
  feel jarring.
- **Mobile-first**. The wordmark needs to work at ~140px wide.
- **Accessibility**: AA contrast minimum on every brand application.
- **No emojis** in any final brand assets or copy.
